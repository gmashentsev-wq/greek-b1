28% churn in Month 1 — the primary value leak
Trial cancellations and impulse churn. Fixing onboarding by 5pp = +$3–5M annual revenue at zero incremental UAC cost. Highest-ROI retention intervention.
Month 12→13 renewal cliff: −26% spike
Annual plan holders who don't renew. Proactive outreach at Month 11 is a high-ROI retention play for the existing subscriber base.
LTV exceeds CAC within 2 months (iOS/Android)
Even with 28% M1 churn, the unit economics work. LTV 12m = $103 (iOS) vs CAC $39. The business model is sound; the question is optimisation speed.
Two churn rates — both correct
Cohort M1 churn (28%) ≠ stock-flow churn (9–16%). M1 = first month cancellations. Stock-flow = steady-state monthly loss across the full subscriber base. Used differently in LTV vs subscriber model.

Why 2026F ≈ 2025E revenue
2025E includes an anomalous Jan-25 UAC spike ($5.1M in one month, 100% of revenue). That month inflates the 2025E total. The underlying 2026 run-rate without that spike is on a gradual growth trajectory. Web base is declining at T12 UAC levels due to churn exceeding new acquisition.
What changes the Base trajectory
Web churn fix (16.4% → 11%) would add ~$3–5M revenue with no UAC change. Alternatively, 25% higher Web UAC spend would stabilise the Web subscriber base. Either move shifts the output materially toward the Bull Case.

Model Methodology

How the model works.

01 INPUT
Monthly UAC budget
By channel: iOS / Android / Web. Scenario-specific growth rate applied from Sep-25.
02 ACQUISITION
÷ CAC = New subs
UAC / CAC / 1,000 = new subscribers per month per channel.
03 STOCK-FLOW
Subscriber base
Subst = Subst−1 × (1−churn) + new. Churn is scenario-specific per channel.
04 REVENUE
Subs × ARPU → GB
Effective ARPU grows at scenario rate from T12 base. Net revenue applies refund and tax deductions.
05 P&L
GP → Contrib → EBITDA
Store commissions per channel. OpEx ratios (S&M, R&D, G&A) applied as % of Net Revenue.

Effective ARPU vs. ops ARPU
Model uses GB ÷ total subscribers (effective ARPU: iOS $6.77, Web $20.68), not the per-paying-user figure ($34–38). This correctly accounts for annual plan holders who appear in subscriber count but bill once per year.

Appendix · Historical Analysis

15 key observations from 32 months of data.

01
Revenue tripled in two years
$11.3M (2023) → $31.0M (2024) → $39.4M (2025E). Growth driven almost entirely by paid acquisition, not organic.
Growth
02
2025 = first Contribution-positive year
$1.4M positive contribution in Jan–Aug 2025. The business model is self-validating at the unit level for the first time.
Milestone
03
Web: 0% → 66% of revenue in 2 years
Deliberate strategy to bypass Apple/Google store commissions. Web billing: $1.8M (2023) → $15.6M (2024) → $17.6M (8m 2025).
Channel
04
ARPU grew +24% in trailing 12 months
Blended ARPU: $26.95/m (Aug-24) → $38.36/m (Aug-25). Driven by annual plan adoption, repricing, and Web mix shift.
Pricing
05
GP margin expanding: 73% → 85%
Improvement driven by channel mix shift toward Web (6% commission) from iOS (28%). Structurally positive as Web share grows.
Margin
06
28% of subscribers leave in Month 1
First-month churn 28.1% across 20 cohorts. Only 15.5% survive to Month 12. Fixing onboarding by 5pp = +$3–5M at zero UAC cost.
Retention
07
Web churn 2× higher than iOS
Web stock-flow churn: 16.4%/month vs iOS 9.5%. At T12 UAC levels, Web subscriber base is net-declining. This is the #1 lever.
Web
08
eROAS declining: 1.46× → 1.18×
Official 2-year eROAS from ops metrics. Declining trend signals rising CPM or audience saturation. Still positive but approaching threshold.
Efficiency
09
iOS/Android: best 12m eROAS
iOS: 2.62× at 12m, payback 1.6 months. Android: 2.52×. Web: 1.20× at 12m. Mobile channels win on capital efficiency at short horizon.
Unit Econ
10
iOS subscriber base −40% from peak
iOS subs: 262k (Dec-23) → 157k (Aug-25). Either user migration to Web (positive read) or iOS retention issue (negative read).
iOS
11
UAC % of revenue: volatile, not a ratio
Range: 39% (Mar-25) to 100% (Jan-25) in 12 months. Not a structural ratio — it's a budget decision that shows up as a metric.
UAC
12
US = 75% of revenue; AU highest ARPU
US at $37.00/m ARPU (+2.1pp share). Australia: 3.8% share, $38.93 ARPU — highest globally. DE growing +1.3pp. RU declining −3.1pp.
Geography
13
Seasonality: Jan peak, Mar trough
Jan-25: $5.1M revenue (UAC = 100% of rev). Mar-25: $2.0M. New Year resolution cycle drives pattern. Affects annual comparisons.
Seasonality
14
Business is profitable "at will"
If UAC reduced to 35% of revenue, EBITDA margin ≈ +20%. Negative EBITDA is a strategic choice. The unit economics work.
Strategy
15
R&D: avg 14% of revenue, range 8–33%
Large project-based spikes. Industry benchmark at scale: 10–15%. Question for Board: what product bets are being funded and what is their retention ROI?
